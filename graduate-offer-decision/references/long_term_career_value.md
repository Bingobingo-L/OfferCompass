# Long-Term Career Value

## Purpose

Calibrate the decision using 2-5 year career value.

Do not predict which company will win. Judge whether the experience is likely to compound into valuable, transferable, market-recognized capabilities.

## What AI Changes

AI increases:

```text
small-team leverage
prototype speed
automation of routine execution
value of compound talent
fluidity between product, engineering, operations, data, and sales
```

AI does not remove:

```text
importance of training quality
importance of direct team and manager
cash-flow constraints
need for real customers and revenue
resume signal
organization and distribution advantages
```

## Startup Opportunities

Startup advantages:

```text
closer to core problems
more from-zero-to-one exposure
higher output visibility
wider responsibility boundary
faster use of new tools
large upside if company and role are right
```

Startup risks:

```text
AI hype
demo without product
trial users without paying customers
weak moat
unstable financing
new graduates used as cheap execution labor
```

Better signals:

```text
real customers > only financing story
continued usage > demo
clear use case > vague platform language
revenue or willingness to pay > vanity user growth
core role > peripheral execution
```

## Big-Company Advantages

Big-company advantages:

```text
data, compute, customers, distribution, brand
mature engineering systems
high-quality peers
new-graduate training
resume signal
lower immediate failure risk
```

Risks:

```text
core AI roles are highly competitive
ordinary roles can be far from core business
organization change and layoffs still happen
individual impact can be low
process can slow learning
```

Better signals:

```text
core business or core AI team > edge support role
real training and strong team > empty platform halo
transferable capability > internal process-only work
```

## Compounding Skills

Evaluate whether the offer builds:

```text
problem definition
AI tool leverage
engineering or product fundamentals
business understanding
customer understanding
data judgment
cross-functional collaboration
learning speed
communication and influence
```

## Role Resilience

More resilient roles:

```text
judgment-heavy
customer- or business-close
AI-augmented
measurable output
transferable skill set
ownership of outcomes
```

Lower-resilience roles:

```text
repetitive execution
low-context processing
internal process only
no business ownership
hard-to-explain output
dependent on one narrow internal platform
```

## Common Paths

```text
big-company core role: training, brand, peers, later move to startup or promotion
big-company edge role: stable but weaker skill and resume story
mature startup core role: moderate risk, good ownership if business is real
early startup core role: high upside and high downside, needs strong support capacity
early startup non-core role: high risk without matching growth, usually weak for new graduates
```
