# Role Team Research And Evaluation

## Purpose

Evaluate the actual role and likely team value, not only the role title.

For a new graduate, the first manager and real project often matter more than company-level narrative.

## Research Targets

Search and infer:

```text
same-company same-role JD
same-company adjacent-role JD
historical hiring posts
business-line or department evidence
employee public profiles
technical blogs and product posts
interview reports
team growth or shrinkage signals
similar roles at comparable companies
```

## Role Reality

Evaluate:

```text
what the person likely does day to day
first 3-6 month deliverables if inferable
core output vs support or execution work
ownership level
success metrics
customer, user, data, system, or product exposure
clarity of responsibilities
```

Red flags:

```text
fancy title but vague duties
"do everything" without ownership
high-pressure business metric directly assigned to a new graduate
all junior team with no senior density
role repeatedly posted for a long time
role only supports internal process
```

## Team Quality

Evaluate:

```text
core business line or edge team
team size and structure
senior member density
new-graduate carrying capacity
peer quality
project history
public technical or product output
team stability
employee exits
```

## Manager Or Leadership Visibility

If public manager information exists, evaluate:

```text
background
domain fit
public work
team-building record
newcomer training signal
clarity of business judgment
```

If unavailable, do not ask the user to go collect it as the main workflow. Convert low manager visibility into lower confidence and higher role risk.

## Training Value

Evaluate whether the role builds:

```text
engineering or product fundamentals
complex problem decomposition
customer or user understanding
data judgment
cross-functional collaboration
delivery discipline
feedback loops
business ownership
```

## Resume Value

Ask:

```text
Can this role produce a clear resume story?
Can project impact be described externally?
Are outputs measurable?
Does the role signal a valuable direction?
Is the skill set transferable?
```
